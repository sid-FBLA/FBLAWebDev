Frequently asked questions

What makes Total Row Fitness unique compared to other fitness centers?
Total Row classes are rowing-centric, making them low-impact, total body, and effective! With small classes and certified Coaches, our program offers a local community vibe with professional quality. Every class is scalable to any age or ability, making Total Row classes available to anyone!
Do I need previous fitness and rowing experience prior to joining Total Row Fitness?
Nope! Our qualified instructors will guide you through each workout regardless of how many classes you've taken.
Are Total Row Fitness classes effective?
Absolutely! Whether you are looking to lose weight, gain muscle, or work on your cardio, we have a program that will help you succeed!
Why did Total Row Fitness decide to model its fitness centers on rowing machines?
Many years ago, when Elliot and Lauren Smith decided to open up the first Total Row center, they wanted to provide members with a safer way to exercise after sustaining multiple injuries in the past. The result was a series of workouts that not only got rid of the incovenience of big box gyms, but are something to look forward to!
How long is each class?
Each class at Total Row is 30 minutes long. TR30 is 30 minutes of rowing intervals, HIIT strength training, and fast-paced fun. We incorporate different tools such as kettle bells, suspension bands, and the always-fun slam balls, to keep the workout fun and challenging.
How can rowing alone help me achieve my fitness goals?
As a single exercise movement, rowing is unmatched in its effectiveness and efficiency. Not only is rowing an amazing cardio exercise, it targets 9 of 11 major muscle groups with each stroke (that’s about 90% of your body!). Members should expect to burn up to 900 calories per hour.Our diverse rowing program will drastically improve your cardio, while driving sustained weight loss. Our tactical strength training will help you get stronger in all the right areas. Our low impact approach will help you recover from injury, or add the perfect cross-training element to any fitness plan.
